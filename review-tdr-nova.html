TDR Nova Review: Surgical Free Dynamic EQ

Why this transparent parallel dynamic equalizer needs to be on every single master channel.

Tokyo Dawn Records is renowned for releasing incredibly high-quality, mastering-grade plugins for free. Among their impressive catalog, **TDR Nova** stands out as the crown jewel: a four-band parallel dynamic equalizer that gives you absolute surgical control over your mix.

What is Dynamic EQ?

Standard equalizers apply a static cut or boost to a frequency. A dynamic EQ, like TDR Nova, only cuts or boosts when the audio passing through it crosses a certain volume threshold. This means if you have a harsh vocal that only sounds piercing during loud notes, TDR Nova will beautifully duck those harsh frequencies precisely when they happen, keeping the rest of the performance bright and present.

Transparent Mastering Interface

TDR Nova shines on the master bus. Its interface features a spectrum analyzer overlay that allows you to easily identify resonant peaks. Moreover, it features parallel processing built directly into the UI, letting you apply heavy dynamic control and blend it seamlessly with the dry signal for absolute transparency.

Why It Is Essential

If you don't want to spend $200 on FabFilter Pro-Q 3, TDR Nova is your absolute best alternative for surgical corrections. Whether you are taming a boomy acoustic guitar, de-essing a siblant vocal, or glueing an entire mix together, Nova handles it with zero artifacting and a very light CPU footprint.

Our Verdict

The ultimate problem-solver plugin. If you mix your own beats, this is mandatory.
